A MUST VISIT PLACE!

Nusa Dua is a resort area built in the 1970s in the southern part of Bali, Indonesia. Known as an enclave of large five-star resorts, it covers 350 hectares of land and encloses more than 20 resorts.

Nusa Dua

Nusa Dua is a promontory located in the southeast of the island of Bali. The distance is approximately 40 kilometers from Denpasar, the capital city of Bali province. Nusa Dua is also the location of the 2008 United Nations Conference on Climate Change between 3 December and 14 December 2007 and the venue for the world's oldest beauty pageant, Miss World 2013.

Nusa Dua Beach

Nusa Dua Beach is located in the Indonesia Tourism & Development Corporation (ITDC) district where large waves of sea water from the Indian Ocean continually crash against jagged limestone edges of a clif

Tanjung Benoa

Immediately north of the Nusa Dua enclave is the peninsula of Tanjung Benoa, which includes cheaper hotels as well as Benoa village. A multi-denominational area, it includes a mosque and Chinese and Hindu temples.
